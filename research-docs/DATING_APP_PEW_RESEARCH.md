# Pew Research Center: Dating App Data

**Last Updated:** December 20, 2025
**Purpose:** Lower-priority academic validation for Dating App Despair metric
**Source:** Pew Research Center + supplementary surveys

---

## Executive Summary

**Key Findings:**
- **45% of dating app users feel frustrated** vs 28% hopeful
- **79% of Gen Z** report "dating app burnout"
- **80% of women** feel burnout (vs 74% of men)
- Only **12% of online daters** end up in committed relationships
- **30% of U.S. adults** have used dating apps (53% of 18-29 year-olds)

**Validation:** Strongly supports Dating App Despair metric - widespread frustration, exhaustion, and low success rates.

---

## User Satisfaction & Emotional Impact

### Overall Experience

**Mixed reviews:**
- **53%** described their experience as positive (very or somewhat)
- **46%** described their experience as negative (very or somewhat)

**Emotional outcomes (past year users):**
- **45% felt frustrated** (dominant emotion)
- **28% felt hopeful**
- **Frustration outweighs hope by 17 percentage points**

### Dating App Burnout

**Forbes Health Survey findings:**

**Gen Z:**
- **79%** reported feeling "dating app burnout"
- Highest burnout rate of any generation

**Gender breakdown:**
- Women: **80%** feel burnout
- Men: **74%** feel burnout
- Women 6 points higher than men

**Implications:** Widespread exhaustion, regardless of demographics

---

## Gender Differences

### Message Volume & Overwhelm

**Women:**
- **Much more likely** to feel overwhelmed by message volume
- **56%** say it's hard to find someone who meets expectations (vs 35% of men)
- **65%** say it's hard to find someone looking for same type of relationship (vs 45% of men)

**Men:**
- **Much more likely** to feel insecure
- **61%** say they don't receive enough messages from people they're interested in (vs 44% of women)

**Analysis:** Women face quantity problems, men face scarcity problems. Both are frustrated, but for opposite reasons.

## Age Differences

### Usage by Age

| Age Group | Usage Rate |
|-----------|-----------|
| 18-29 | **53%** |
| 30-49 | **37%** |

**Pattern:** Dating app usage drops sharply with age

### Motivations by Age

**Older adults (40+):**
- **71% of women** aren't looking to date right now
- **42% of men** aren't looking to date right now
- Women 40+ significantly less interested than men

---

## Success Rates

### Relationship Outcomes

**Long-term success:**
- Only **12%** of online daters married or entered committed relationship with someone they met online
- **88% did not** find long-term partner

**Meeting partners online (all couples, not just app users):**
- **1 in 10 partnered adults** met current partner on site/app
- **1 in 5 partnered adults under 30** met current partner on site/app

**Analysis:** Success rates are low overall, but younger couples more likely to have met online

---

## Safety & Negative Experiences

### Unwanted Behaviors

**48%** of dating app users report experiencing at least one unwanted behavior:
- Harassment
- Unsolicited explicit messages
- Threatening messages
- Stalking behaviors
- Catfishing

### Financial Scams

**Romance scams (2023):**
- **$1.14 billion** in losses
- Dating apps create vulnerability to financial exploitation

## Overall Dating Climate

### General Dating Difficulty

**Across demographics:**

**Americans' view on dating difficulty (2020):**
- **Nearly half of U.S. adults** say dating has gotten harder in the last 10 years

## Implications for Dating App Despair Metric

### Official Score Component (Currently Missing)

**Potential metrics to add:**
1. **Frustration rate:** 45% feel frustrated
2. **Burnout rate:** 79% (Gen Z), 80% (women) average ~77%
3. **Success rate (inverse):** Only 12% succeed → 88% failure rate

**Suggested normalized score:**
```
Dating difficulty = (frustration_rate + burnout_rate + failure_rate) / 3
                  = (45 + 77 + 88) / 3
                  = 70.0
```

**Interpretation:** 70/100 indicates severe dysfunction in modern dating

### Social Score Validation

**Pew data confirms YouTube findings:**
- YouTube crisis ratio: **31.55%** (Level 2+3)
- Pew frustration: **45%**
- Pew burnout: **77-80%**

**Strong correlation** between social media sentiment and academic survey data

## Demographic Insights

### Who's Most Affected?

**High burnout groups:**
1. Gen Z (79%)
2. Women (80%)
3. Young adults 18-29 (53% usage, high frustration)

**Lower impact groups:**
1. Adults 50+ (16% usage)
2. Older women (71% not looking to date)

### Gender Asymmetry

**The paradox:**
- Women overwhelmed by **too many** low-quality matches
- Men frustrated by **too few** matches
- Both feel **equally burned out**
- Neither is satisfied

**Result:** System fails both genders, just differently

## Integration Plan

### Option 1: Add Pew Data to Official Score

**Current:** Dating App Despair has no official score component (0% weight)

**Proposed:**
- Official score: Pew frustration rate (45%) + success failure rate (88%) = avg 66.5
- Social score: YouTube crisis ratio (31.55%)
- **Final score:** (66.5 × 0.4) + (31.55 × 0.6) = 26.6 + 18.9 = **45.5**

**Impact:** Would increase score from 31.55 → 45.5 (+44% increase)

### Option 2: Use Pew as Validation Only

- Keep current social-only score (31.55)
- Reference Pew data in methodology as validation
- Note: "Academic research confirms widespread frustration (45%) and burnout (79%)"

**Recommendation:** **Option 1** - Add Pew data to official score component for more robust measurement
